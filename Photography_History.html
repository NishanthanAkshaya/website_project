The First Camera

Aristotle used the principles of a camera obscura in 4th century BC‚ where an image is projected through a small hole. However‚ a scientist named Alhazen (or Ibn al-Haytham) is said to have actually invented the camera obscura and pinole camera through his extensive study of how images are formed by light. Although the camera was revolutionary:

  1. It only created upside-down images
  2. It was very large and not very portable

On the other hand‚ its size lead to many observatories were set-up for groups to look at pictures through the camera and was later refined to add a lens to focus light and made smaller and more portable.

Recording Images

Joseph Nicéphore Niépce and Louis Jacques Mandé Daguerre created the basis for the cameras we are familiar with today. Joseph Niépce experimented with silver chloride and silver halide but the recorded images darkened with light. In 1862‚ he used a sheet of pewter coated with bitumen which needed to be exposed for 8 hours.

Louis Daguerre‚ a painter refined the image-creating process ith silver-plate sheets of copper fumed with mercury vapour. By 1939‚ Daguerre reduced exposure time to mere seconds‚ which meant this camera model was used for commercial portraits. Willam Henry Fox invented the calotype processa few years later‚ allowing photographers to create negatives where multiples prints could be created. In 1851‚ Frederick Scott Archer invented the collodion wet plate process, which produced a negative image on a transparent glass plate and was used for tintype portraits until around the 1900s. Gelatin dry plate process was introduced in the late 1800s and was used more often.

Shaping Modern-Day Photography

In 1889‚ George Eastman created the roll of film‚ allowing people to concequitively shoot multiple pictures. His company Kodak allowed snapshots to be made and there wasn’t a need to individually process each picture. Thomas Edision added the 35mm format and Oskar Barnack introduced the compact Leica camera with 35 mm film.

Kodak then released Kodachrome with multiple layers tha created colour film. When World War II started in 1939, photojournalists used the new lens to capture the realities of war instead of creating staged pictures.

Refining the Camera

Around this time‚ Polaroid introduced instant images‚ but stopped producing their instant film in 2008. Asahi (later Pentax) introduced the Asahiflex and Nikon introduced the Nikon F camera‚ SLR cameras‚ in the 1950s. SLR cameras are single-lens reflex cameras that use a mirror and prism system so that the user can see what they’re going to take a picture of. These allowed interchangeable lenses and other customizations to be made.

From the 1950s to and early ‘80s, cameras that could calculate shutter speed, aperture, and focus were created and the what was considered the first self-contained digitsal camer was made by Kodak 1975. By 1991‚ Kodak created the first fully digital camera taht was good enough for professional use and other manufacturers started introducing advanced digital(DSLR) cameras.
